## Opening (2026-07-08) — why G1

The Ghost Geometry program has a sharp local machine (residue
automaton, death shell, capacity edge, spectral certificates) and a
stubborn global gap: **corridor exits do not automatically compose
into a proof that deficit stays bounded** for every positive integer.
F5 (358 vs 359 at C=148) is an important law-fork inside the local
machine; it is not the whole war.

G1 is a deliberately small campaign by a new agent (Grok) on this
repo's discipline:

1. **Freeze intent** in `IMPLEMENTATION_PLAN.md` (predictions included).
2. **Run with gates.**
3. **Receipt everything** in the ledger.
4. **Tell the story here.**

Two experiments, independent:

- **Letter surgery** asks whether the support-cost story (one letter at
  scale ~358 flipping support→drop) is the kind of thing the D(m)
  instrument actually hears — without pretending we can enumerate
  \(m=359\).
- **Exit toll** asks whether real upward breaches prefer the death
  shell's skin — the empirical shadow of a composition law.

If exit toll dies, the next track should pivot harder into
integer-realizability / dual-game ideas, not more corridor
bookkeeping. If letter surgery dies, W6D-M style F5 routes need
re-operationalization. Either outcome is progress.
