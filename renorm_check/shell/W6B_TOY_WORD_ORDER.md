# W6B Work Order — Toy-Word Mechanism Experiment (registered 2026-07-03, Fable)

**Context:** W6's transducer route FAILED at E1 (follower sets do not
stabilize in raw coordinates — see IMPLEMENTATION_LEDGER, authoritative
E1 run). This order replaces it with a direct mechanism experiment that
needs no transducer and no extrapolation.

**Question:** does corridor capacity follow the driving word's
IRRATIONAL slope, or pin to the word's RATIONAL convergent? This is the
358-vs-359 mechanism question — tested on a system whose divergence
points are densely computable.

**Construction.** The real automaton's credit word is
c_k = ⌊(k+1)α⌋ − ⌊kα⌋ with α = log₂3. Build the IDENTICAL automaton —
same residue map (3r+1)·2^(−a) mod 3^m, same corridor/deficit rules,
same `run_heartbeat` machinery — with the word slope replaced by
**α_toy = φ = (1+√5)/2**. Only the credit sequence changes.

- Exact credits are LAW (mirror of W1): ⌊kφ⌋ = (k + isqrt(5·k²)) // 2
  (Python `math.isqrt`, arbitrary precision, no floats). Cross-check
  against 60-digit Decimal for k = 0..100000 before any measurement.
- Implement as a variant `credit_toy(k)` alongside (never replacing)
  `automaton.credit`; the automaton code is shared, parameterized by
  the credit function.

**Why φ:** its credit-word slope of 1s is β_toy = 2−φ ≈ 0.382, with
convergents the Fibonacci ratios (1/2, 2/5, 3/8, 5/13, 8/21). The
divergence points between ⌈β_toy·m − 1⌉ and its convergent shadows
land at SMALL m — inside the dense ceiling (m ≤ 13 at C=12) — where
the real system's first divergence (m=359) is unreachable.

## T1 — Port sanity (controls first)

Reuse the pipeline with the TRUE word (α = log₂3) and confirm it
reproduces the known results bit-for-bit: D(m) for m=2..12 (SYNTHESIS
F8 / shell_probe P5) and the C=1..5 edges. Same code path, only the

## T2 — Toy shell survey

With `credit_toy`: heredity check (must hold — the one-line proof is
word-independent), universality check across C ∈ {8, 12, 23} at
m = 2..6 (expected to hold; report if not — that would itself be a
finding), and the dead-shell profile vs m at C=12.

## T3 — The measurement

D_toy(m) for m = 2..13 (dense, wide corridor, min ceiling-distance of
a live terminal, exactly as shell_probe P5). Before computing ANY
D_toy values: tabulate the candidate laws —
  irrational: ⌈β_toy·m − 1⌉ (exact integer test: k ≥ (2−φ)m − 1 ⟺
  algebraic manipulation via isqrt, no floats)
  convergent shadows: ⌈(p/q)·m − 1⌉ for p/q ∈ {1/2, 2/5, 3/8, 5/13}
and list every m ≤ 13 where any two candidates differ. Those are the
decisive rows. Small constant offsets between D_toy and the laws are
permitted if CONSTANT across the agreement region (fit on agreeing m,
frozen before reading the decisive rows); pattern of divergence is
the readout, not the absolute value.

## T4 — Readout and registration

For each decisive m: which law does D_toy match? Report per-row, no
aggregation that hides a mixed result. A mixed result (some rows
slope, some convergent) is reported as mixed — that would be the most
important outcome of all.

**Registered prediction (Fable, 2026-07-03): D_toy follows the
IRRATIONAL golden slope at every decisive row.** If it pins to a
convergent, that prediction is wrong, gets recorded as wrong, and
materially weakens the registered F5 prediction (359) — the same
mechanism argument supports both. Asymmetry, stated pre-data: a
slope-following toy SUPPORTS but cannot prove 359 (the real word's α
is generated by the arithmetic's own 2 and 3 — a resonance unique to
the true coupling remains possible); a convergent-pinned toy is
STRONG evidence against 359.
